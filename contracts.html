Understanding Contracts 101

Contract Essentials

Every dance contract should clearly outline:

  • Parties involved and their roles
  • Scope of work and deliverables
  • Compensation details and payment schedule
  • Intellectual property rights
  • Cancellation and termination clauses
  • Liability and insurance provisions

Red Flags to Avoid

  • Vague payment terms or "exposure-only" deals
  • Contracts without cancellation clauses
  • Broad image rights that limit future work
  • No liability protection for the performer
  • Unclear ownership of creative work
  • Last-minute contract changes

Your Rights as a Dancer

  • Right to fair compensation for your work
  • Right to safe working conditions
  • Right to retain copyright of your choreography
  • Right to negotiate contract terms
  • Right to legal representation
  • Right to terminate unsafe agreements

Negotiation Tips & Strategies

1

Know Your Worth

Research industry rates for your experience level and location. Don't undervalue your skills and time.

2

Read Everything

Never sign without reading the entire contract. Ask questions about unclear terms before agreeing.

3

Document Changes

All contract modifications should be in writing. Verbal agreements are hard to enforce legally.

4

Set Boundaries

Clearly define what's included and excluded in your services. Scope creep can hurt your business.

5

Payment Terms

Always request deposits for larger gigs and set clear payment deadlines with late fee clauses.

6

Exit Strategy

Include fair cancellation terms for both parties. Life happens, and contracts should account for this.
